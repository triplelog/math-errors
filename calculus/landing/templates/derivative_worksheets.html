Four

Sum Rule and Constant Multiples

The easiest rule in Calculus is the sum rule so make sure you understand it.

  • Quick Refresher
    Differentiate each term
    Separate the function into its terms and find the derivative of each term. Then add up the derivatives. Also applies to subtraction in the same way. Constant multiples are a specific case of the sum rule. $$\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)+g(x)]=\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]+\frac{d}{dx}[g(x)]$$ $$\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)-g(x)]=\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]-\frac{d}{dx}[g(x)]$$ $$\frac{d}{dx}[cf(x)]=c\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]$$

Five

Product Rule

Products are not as nice as sums, so spend some time practicing how to keep track of all the functions.

  • Quick Refresher
    Determine the factors
    Rewrite the function as the product of two simpler functions. Then differentiate these two functions and combine them as dictated by the product rule. $$\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)]=f(x)\frac{d}{dx}[g(x)]+g(x)\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]$$

Six

Quotient Rule

The more quotient rule problems you work the more natural it will be and you won't have to second guess your memory.

  • Quick Refresher
    Differentiate Fractions
    Rewrite the function as a fraction with numerator and denominator each dependent on $x$. Differentiate the numerator and denominator and input these derivatives into thte quotient rule. $$\frac{d}{dx}[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}]=\frac{g(x)\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]-f(x)\frac{d}{dx}[g(x)]}{g(x)^2}$$

Seven

Chain Rule

The chain rule needs to be explored with practice so get to work on it right now.

  • Quick Refresher
    Undo Function Composition
    First, determine the outside function. Then what is left over is the inside function. Determine the derivative of the outside and inside functions and input into the chain rule. $$\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]=\frac{d}{dg(x)}[f(g(x))]\frac{d}{dx}[g(x)]$$
